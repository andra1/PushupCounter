How abstract should the animation be?

Each approach uses the same underlying pose/angle data from your camera pipeline

A. Stick Figure Replay

Simplified skeleton replays the actual pushup motion. Green-to-red color shift shows form degradation over the set. You literally see the range of motion shrink.

Pros

  • Immediately readable
  • Shows exact form
  • Feels personal

Cons

  • Requires saving full pose keypoints
  • More data per session

B. Effort Waveform

A pulsing waveform where each peak is a rep. Amplitude = depth of the pushup, frequency = tempo. Color shifts green → red as form degrades. Animates left to right like an EKG.

Pros

  • Compact & iconic (like Strava's map)
  • Only needs angle + timestamps
  • Unique per session

Cons

  • More abstract, less immediately obvious
  • Needs a legend or context

C. Hybrid: Figure + Data

Stick figure replay with angle arcs drawn at the elbow, plus a mini waveform strip at the bottom showing the full session at a glance. Both animate together.

Pros

  • Best of both worlds
  • Rich but still compact
  • Educational — shows what "good form" means

Cons

  • Most complex to build
  • Requires full pose keypoints
  • Might be visually busy
